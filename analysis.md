        <h2>About the Project</h2>
        <p>
            This Digital Humanities project explores temporary architecture in India as a powerful yet often overlooked form of cultural expression. From festival pandals and protest sites to ceremonial structures and transient installations, these ephemeral forms reshape urban landscapes while carrying deep social, political, and aesthetic meanings.
        </p>

        <p>
            Rather than treating these structures as disposable, this project approaches them as dynamic cultural texts. Their brief existence allows us to examine how space is continuously produced, inhabited, and remembered, revealing layers of meaning that persist even after physical disappearance.
        </p>

        <h2>Core Focus</h2>
        <p>
            The project investigates how temporary architectures act as sites of performance, memory, and spatial transformation. It asks how these structures intervene in everyday life and how their absence continues to shape cultural memory.
        </p>

        <h2>Research Questions</h2>
        <ul>
            <li>How does temporary architecture reflect cultural and political life in India?</li>
            <li>What meanings are embedded in materials, design, and spatial arrangement?</li>
            <li>How do these structures transform and redefine public space?</li>
            <li>What remains after they disappear, and how is memory constructed?</li>
        </ul>

        <h2>Theoretical Framework</h2>
        <p>
            This project draws on interdisciplinary approaches to understand the complexity of ephemeral space:
        </p>

        <ul>
            <li><strong>Production of Space:</strong> Understanding space as socially created and continuously negotiated.</li>
            <li><strong>Performance Theory:</strong> Viewing temporary structures as performative events shaped by participation and temporality.</li>
            <li><strong>Memory Studies:</strong> Exploring how absence, documentation, and recollection sustain these structures beyond their material life.</li>
        </ul>

        <h2>Why It Matters</h2>
        <p>
            In rapidly changing urban environments, temporary architectures reveal alternative ways of inhabiting space. They challenge permanence, foreground collective experience, and offer insight into how culture is continuously made and remade in the public sphere.
        </p>
